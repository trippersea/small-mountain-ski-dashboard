Epic Pass vs Ikon Pass:
The Complete Regional Guide

The Epic Pass and Ikon Pass cover hundreds of ski resorts across North America, but they rarely overlap. The right pass for you depends entirely on where you ski. We break down every covered mountain by region so you can make the right call before prices go up.

Frequently asked questions

What is the difference between the Epic Pass and the Ikon Pass?

The Epic Pass is operated by Vail Resorts and covers resorts including Vail, Breckenridge, Park City, Stowe, and Whistler Blackcomb. The Ikon Pass is operated by Alterra Mountain Company and covers resorts including Mammoth Mountain, Jackson Hole, Aspen Snowmass, Alta, Snowbird, and Palisades Tahoe. The two passes cover different mountains with very little overlap, so the best choice depends on which specific resorts you want to ski.

Which is cheaper, the Epic Pass or the Ikon Pass?

Both passes are priced in a similar range, typically between $700 and $1,000 per adult for the full unrestricted version with early purchase pricing. Both offer lower-cost restricted versions: the Epic Local Pass and the Ikon Base Pass limit access to certain resorts or add blackout dates during peak periods. For current season pricing, check the official Epic Pass and Ikon Pass websites directly before purchasing.

Can I use both the Epic Pass and the Ikon Pass at the same resort?

No. Epic Pass and Ikon Pass resorts are operated by competing companies. A single resort is only included on one pass network. To ski resorts on both networks in the same season, you would need to purchase both passes or pay day ticket prices at resorts not covered by your pass.

Is the Indy Pass worth considering compared to Epic and Ikon?

The Indy Pass covers over 100 independent ski resorts not affiliated with either the Epic or Ikon networks. It is significantly cheaper than both major passes and works best for skiers who prefer smaller, uncrowded, independently owned mountains. Many skiers combine an Epic or Ikon Pass with an Indy Pass to gain access to local hills not covered by either major network.

How do I decide between the Epic Pass and the Ikon Pass?

Start by listing the specific resorts you plan to ski next season, then check which pass covers them. If your priority resorts are split across both networks, determine which pass covers more of your top choices. Consider your home region: the Ikon Pass is generally stronger in Vermont (outside Stowe), Utah's Cottonwood Canyons, the Pacific Northwest, and California's Eastern Sierra. The Epic Pass is stronger for New Hampshire, Park City, Vail, and several Lake Tahoe resorts.

When should I buy the Epic Pass or Ikon Pass to get the best price?

Both passes offer the lowest prices during spring early bird sales, typically running from late March through early June before the following ski season. Prices increase in stages as the season approaches. Buying in the spring window usually saves between $100 and $300 per adult compared to purchasing in the fall. Both passes also offer refund protection options for an additional fee in case your plans change.
