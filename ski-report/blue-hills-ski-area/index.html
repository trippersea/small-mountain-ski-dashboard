Blue Hills at a glance

When the goal is turns without a huge bill, Blue Hills is worth a look. 309 feet of vertical for roughly $69 per day.

With 90" average snow, this is built around guns and grooming more than storms. 6,000 GPM of snowmaking backs up the natural average, and the crowd picture is simple: it the kind of hill where you recognize faces by February.

Day tickets land around $69, depending on date and demand. Works when it is in your backyard, not when you are driving past better options.

Best for
  • beginners and learning skiers
  • budget-conscious skiers
  • after-work or night skiing sessions
Not ideal for
  • skiers who need variety over a full day
  • natural snow seekers. Snowmaking does most of the work
  • explorers who want to cover new terrain all day
When to go

January and February is peak season. December is hit or miss. The base needs to build first. Weekdays are noticeably quieter if you can swing it.
