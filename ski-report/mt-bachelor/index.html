Mt. Bachelor — At a Glance

Mt. Bachelor is the kind of place you plan a trip around. With 3,365 feet of vertical drop across 101 trails and 4,318 skiable acres, there is enough mountain here to keep you exploring for days without repeating yourself. You will find everything from long groomers that let you open it up to steeps that make you stop and think for a second before committing. The base area has that full resort feel — food, après options, and things to do once the lifts stop spinning.

At 9,065 feet at the summit, Mt. Bachelor gets an average of 462 inches of snow per year, which is exceptional — this is a legitimate powder destination. That snowfall average, combined with its natural snowfall base, means the mountain can maintain solid coverage through most of the season. Crowds are part of the deal at a resort this size — it busy enough on weekends but manageable if you pick your days. That said, the scale of the mountain makes it absolutely worth a longer drive when conditions are right. Indy Pass access is included, which is worth factoring in if you're already a pass holder.

When to go

January through March is peak season. December can be good if the season starts early. Midweek is almost always better than weekends, especially for pass holders when lift lines stack up.
