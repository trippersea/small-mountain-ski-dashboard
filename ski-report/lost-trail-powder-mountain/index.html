Lost Trail Powder Mountain at a glance

Lost Trail Powder Mountain hits a sweet spot a lot of regional skiers like: 1,800 feet of vertical across 68 trails without the destination-resort overhead. Snow averages 300 inches, which is solid enough that storms actually change the day. Coverage leans on what falls from the sky. Crowds enough people to keep lifts spinning, not enough to ruin a lap. Worth a dedicated day when snow is in the forecast. Indy Pass holders ski here as part of the pass. Day tickets run about $49 if you are buying walk-up.

Best for
  • intermediate skiers looking to progress
  • Indy pass holders
  • budget-conscious skiers
  • powder chasers
Not ideal for

When to go

January through March is peak season. December can be good if the season starts early. Midweek is almost always better than weekends, especially for pass holders when lift lines stack up.
