Breckenridge — At a Glance

Breckenridge is a ski resort in Colorado, rising from 9,600 ft to 12,998 ft with 3,398 ft of vertical drop. The mountain offers 187 trails across 2,908 skiable acres — 14% beginner, 31% intermediate, and 55% advanced terrain.

With an average of 300" of snowfall per season and 600 GPM of snowmaking capacity, Breckenridge can typically maintain good coverage throughout the winter. Breckenridge skews toward experienced skiers — 55% of the mountain is advanced terrain. The mountain features a terrain park.

Breckenridge is a Epic Pass mountain. Day tickets start around $240 — prices vary by date and demand. For live snow conditions, drive-time from your location, and a personalized Ski Score, use WhereToSkiNext.com.
