Berkshire East — At a Glance

Berkshire East sits in that sweet spot a lot of skiers gravitate toward. With 1,180 feet of vertical and 46 trails spread across 315 acres, it is big enough to give you genuine variety without feeling overwhelming. You can get into a rhythm here — lap a favorite chair, work on something specific, and still have new terrain to find by afternoon.

Snow averages 90 inches per season, which is limited — snowmaking is the main reason this place stays open, and the 15,000 GPM snowmaking operation helps keep coverage consistent when nature does not fully cooperate. Lift lines tend to move here. It is the kind of mountain where you actually ski most of the day instead of standing in queues, though weekends can get busy during the holiday stretch. Window tickets run around $80. Indy Pass access is included, which is worth factoring in if you're already a pass holder.

Best for
  • Indy pass holders
  • budget-conscious skiers
  • after-work or night skiing sessions
Not ideal for
  • natural snow seekers — snowmaking does most of the work
When to go

January and February is peak season. December is hit or miss — the base needs to build first. Midweek is almost always better than weekends, especially for pass holders when lift lines stack up.
