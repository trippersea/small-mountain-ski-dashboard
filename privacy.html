Privacy Policy

Your data stays yours.

WhereToSkiNext.com doesn't sell your data, doesn't require an account, and stores as little as possible — most of it never leaves your browser.

Last updated: June 2026

The short version

WhereToSkiNext.com doesn't sell your data and doesn't require an account. If you sign up for the newsletter, your email goes to Beehiiv (our email platform). If you use the AI assistant or filters, anonymous usage events are logged to help improve the site. Your precise location (the coordinates used to calculate drive times) is never stored on our servers. A coarse label like "Boston, MA" is saved with anonymous analytics if you use location features, and your full settings are encoded into a share link only if you create one. We use Google Analytics for aggregate traffic data. That's the whole picture.

Page analytics Aggregate traffic data — page views, feature usage, traffic sources. Google Analytics / GTM servers. See Google's privacy policy below.

We do not collect your name, payment information, or any account credentials. There are no accounts. Email is only collected if you choose to subscribe to the newsletter.

WhereToSkiNext.com itself does not set cookies. Google Analytics sets its own cookies (_ga, _gid, etc.) to distinguish unique visitors and sessions.

You can clear all of this at any time by clearing your browser's site data for wheretoskinext.com, or by resetting filters in the app.

Shareable URLs

When you click the "Share" button or share a resort report URL, your current filter settings — including your approximate coordinates and location label — are encoded into the URL. Anyone with that link will be able to see those settings. If you'd prefer not to share your location, clear the location field before copying a share link.

Children's privacy

WhereToSkiNext.com is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect any information from children. Because we don't collect personal information from any user, there is nothing to delete — but if you have concerns, contact us at the address below.

Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to how we handle data, we'll update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We won't start collecting personal data without updating this policy first.

June 2026: Added the crowd feedback prompt, which asks on a later visit whether a mountain we recommended turned out quiet or crowded. It is anonymous, optional, and tied only to a random session ID.
